COMPREHENSIVE GI CARE

Detailed Treatment Information for Digestive Diseases

Dr. Arif Raza evaluates each patient with a focus on accurate diagnosis, clear explanation and the least invasive effective treatment. The services below cover gastrointestinal cancer surgery, liver and pancreatic care, hernia repair, bariatric surgery, proctology, endoscopy and emergency GI surgery.

This page is intended for patient education. A consultation is needed to confirm diagnosis, risks, alternatives and the right treatment plan for an individual condition.

GI Cancer Surgery

GI cancer surgery involves diagnosis, staging and surgical treatment for cancers of the stomach, esophagus, colon, rectum, liver, pancreas, gallbladder and bile duct. Treatment may include biopsy, endoscopy, imaging review, nutrition optimization and coordination with medical or radiation oncology when needed.

Common reasons to consult

  • Unexplained weight loss, anemia, bleeding or bowel habit changes
  • Confirmed stomach, colon, rectal, liver or pancreatic cancer
  • Need for second opinion on operability or surgical planning

Care approach

The goal is complete and safe cancer removal where surgery is appropriate, with careful planning around recovery, nutrition and long-term follow-up.

Advanced Laparoscopic Surgery

Laparoscopic surgery uses small incisions and a camera to treat selected abdominal conditions. It can reduce wound size, pain and hospital stay when compared with traditional open surgery for suitable patients.

Procedures include

  • Laparoscopic gallbladder removal
  • Laparoscopic appendix and hernia surgery
  • Selected colorectal, bariatric and upper GI procedures

Care approach

Suitability depends on diagnosis, previous surgeries, disease severity and patient fitness. Safety remains the deciding factor.

Liver & Pancreatic (HPB) Surgery

HPB surgery focuses on diseases of the liver, pancreas, gallbladder and bile ducts. These conditions often require detailed imaging review and careful planning because they affect digestion, jaundice, infection risk and cancer care.

Conditions treated

  • Gallstones, gallbladder inflammation and bile duct stones
  • Liver cysts, abscesses, tumors and selected metastases
  • Pancreatic tumors, cysts, pancreatitis complications and bile duct obstruction

Care approach

Treatment may involve medication, endoscopy, ERCP, laparoscopic surgery or major HPB surgery depending on the condition.

Hernia Surgery

A hernia occurs when tissue pushes through a weak area in the abdominal wall. Surgery may be recommended when a hernia is painful, enlarging, affecting daily activity or at risk of complications.

Types treated

  • Inguinal, umbilical and epigastric hernias
  • Incisional and recurrent hernias
  • Selected complex abdominal wall hernias

Care approach

Repair may be open or laparoscopic, often with mesh reinforcement. The approach is chosen based on hernia size, location and patient factors.

Bariatric Surgery

Bariatric surgery supports long-term weight loss in carefully selected patients with obesity, especially when lifestyle and medical treatment have not been enough or obesity-related health problems are present.

Evaluation includes

  • BMI, metabolic risk and obesity-related conditions
  • Dietary, psychological and medical fitness assessment
  • Clear discussion of benefits, risks and long-term follow-up needs

Care approach

Surgery is planned with nutrition guidance and lifelong follow-up because sustained results depend on behavior, monitoring and supplementation.

Upper GI Surgery

Upper GI surgery treats conditions involving the esophagus, stomach and first part of the small intestine. Patients may need evaluation for reflux, obstruction, ulcers, tumors or swallowing-related symptoms.

Conditions treated

  • Stomach and esophageal tumors
  • Complicated ulcers, strictures and obstruction
  • Selected reflux and hiatal hernia conditions

Care approach

Endoscopy and imaging help determine whether medication, endoscopic therapy or surgery is the right option.

Colorectal Surgery

Colorectal surgery manages diseases of the colon, rectum and anus. Treatment can range from medical management and endoscopy to laparoscopic or open surgery.

Conditions treated

  • Colon and rectal cancers
  • Diverticular disease, obstruction and bleeding
  • Complex piles, fistula, fissure and abscess problems

Care approach

Planning focuses on disease control, bowel function, continence, recovery and long-term surveillance where required.

Emergency GI Surgery

Emergency GI surgery is required for serious abdominal conditions that cannot safely wait. Early assessment can reduce complications in infections, perforations, bleeding and bowel obstruction.

Emergency problems include

  • Severe abdominal pain, perforation or peritonitis
  • Intestinal obstruction and strangulated hernia
  • Appendicitis, gallbladder infection and abdominal trauma

Care approach

Urgent evaluation, stabilization, imaging and timely surgery are coordinated based on the patient's condition.

Laser Piles & Fistula Surgery

Laser proctology is used for selected anal conditions such as piles, fistula and fissure. It may reduce tissue trauma and support faster recovery in suitable cases.

Symptoms to evaluate

  • Bleeding during stools, swelling or prolapse
  • Painful fissure or recurrent anal abscess
  • Persistent discharge suggesting fistula

Care approach

A clinical examination determines whether medicines, office treatment, laser procedure or conventional surgery is most appropriate.

Upper GI Endoscopy

Upper GI endoscopy allows direct examination of the food pipe, stomach and duodenum using a flexible camera. It helps diagnose acidity-related symptoms, ulcers, bleeding, narrowing and suspected tumors.

Used for

  • Persistent acidity, vomiting, pain or difficulty swallowing
  • Blood vomiting, black stools or anemia evaluation
  • Biopsy, bleeding control and selected therapeutic procedures

Care approach

Findings are explained after the procedure, and biopsy or further treatment is planned when needed.

Colonoscopy

Colonoscopy examines the large intestine and rectum. It is useful for bleeding, chronic bowel changes, unexplained anemia, suspected inflammation and cancer screening or surveillance.

Used for

  • Blood in stool, constipation, diarrhea or abdominal pain
  • Polyp detection and removal when appropriate
  • Follow-up after colorectal cancer or polyp treatment

Care approach

Good bowel preparation is important for accuracy. Results guide medication, surveillance or surgery if required.

ERCP

ERCP is an advanced endoscopic procedure used to diagnose and treat selected bile duct and pancreatic duct problems. It can help remove bile duct stones, place stents and relieve obstruction.

Used for

  • Jaundice caused by bile duct blockage
  • Bile duct stones and cholangitis
  • Selected pancreatic and bile duct strictures or leaks

Care approach

ERCP is recommended only when the expected benefit justifies procedure-related risks, and it is often coordinated with imaging and surgery plans.
