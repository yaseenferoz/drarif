COLON, RECTUM & ANUS

Colorectal Surgery in Kalaburagi

Diagnosis and treatment for colon, rectal and anal conditions including cancers, bleeding, bowel obstruction, inflammatory disease and complex proctology problems.

Colorectal surgery covers diseases of the large intestine, rectum and anus. Symptoms such as bleeding, altered bowel habit and anemia should be assessed early because causes range from piles to cancer.

Treatment planning considers disease control, bowel function, continence and long-term surveillance.

Book Consultation
WHEN TO CONSULT

Symptoms and Situations That Need Review

Blood in stool, black stool or unexplained anemia

New constipation, diarrhea or narrowing of stool

Rectal pain, swelling, discharge or recurrent abscess

Colon or rectal mass, polyp or cancer on colonoscopy

Diagnosis & Evaluation

  • Clinical examination and proctoscopy when required
  • Colonoscopy with biopsy or polyp removal when appropriate
  • CT/MRI staging for cancer and complex fistula
  • Blood tests and stool tests in selected conditions

Treatment Options

  • Laparoscopic or open colon and rectal surgery
  • Cancer surgery with oncology coordination when needed
  • Surgery for obstruction, perforation or severe bleeding
  • Treatment for piles, fissure, fistula and abscess

How to Prepare

  • Report bleeding or bowel habit change early
  • Follow bowel preparation instructions before colonoscopy or surgery
  • Discuss stoma possibility honestly when rectal or emergency surgery is planned

Recovery & Follow-Up

Recovery includes diet progression, bowel function monitoring, wound care and pathology review. Some patients need surveillance colonoscopy or oncology treatment after surgery.

URGENT CARE

Warning Signs That Should Not Wait

  • Heavy rectal bleeding or dizziness
  • Severe abdominal swelling with vomiting
  • Fever with rectal swelling or severe pain
  • Inability to pass stool or gas

This page is for patient education. A physical consultation is required to confirm diagnosis, risks, alternatives and the right treatment plan.
