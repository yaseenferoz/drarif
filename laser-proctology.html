PROCTOLOGY CARE

Laser Piles & Fistula Surgery in Kalaburagi

Evaluation and treatment for piles, fistula, fissure and selected anal conditions, including laser procedures where suitable.

Proctology problems are common but often delayed because patients feel embarrassed. Bleeding, pain, swelling and discharge need proper examination because treatment differs for piles, fissure, fistula and abscess.

Laser treatment can be useful in selected cases, but not every anal condition needs laser or surgery. The right option depends on diagnosis and severity.

Book Consultation
WHEN TO CONSULT

Symptoms and Situations That Need Review

Bleeding during stools or prolapsing piles

Sharp pain while passing stool suggesting fissure

Pus discharge, recurrent swelling or abscess suggesting fistula

Itching, swelling or lump near the anus

Diagnosis & Evaluation

  • Clinical examination and proctoscopy
  • MRI fistulogram for complex or recurrent fistula
  • Colonoscopy if bleeding pattern suggests bowel disease
  • Assessment of constipation and diet habits

Treatment Options

  • Medicines, fiber and stool-softening measures for early disease
  • Laser piles or fistula procedure in selected cases
  • Conventional surgery for complex fistula, abscess or advanced piles
  • Drainage when acute abscess is present

How to Prepare

  • Do not assume all bleeding is piles
  • Avoid repeated self-medication without examination
  • Improve constipation and hydration before and after treatment

Recovery & Follow-Up

Most proctology procedures need wound hygiene, stool-softening, pain control and follow-up. Healing time varies, especially for fistula disease.

URGENT CARE

Warning Signs That Should Not Wait

  • Severe anal pain with fever
  • Heavy bleeding or dizziness
  • Rapidly increasing swelling near anus
  • Persistent discharge after prior fistula surgery

This page is for patient education. A physical consultation is required to confirm diagnosis, risks, alternatives and the right treatment plan.
