BILE DUCT & PANCREATIC DUCT CARE

ERCP in Kalaburagi

Advanced endoscopic procedure for selected bile duct and pancreatic duct problems, including stones, strictures, leaks and obstructive jaundice.

ERCP combines endoscopy and X-ray guidance to diagnose and treat selected bile duct and pancreatic duct problems. It is commonly used when treatment is expected, such as removing bile duct stones or placing a stent.

Because ERCP has specific risks, it is recommended only when the likely benefit is clear.

Book Consultation
WHEN TO CONSULT

Symptoms and Situations That Need Review

Jaundice, dark urine or pale stools

Fever with jaundice suggesting cholangitis

Bile duct stone on ultrasound, CT or MRCP

Pancreatic or bile duct leak, stricture or blockage

Diagnosis & Evaluation

  • Review of liver function tests and imaging such as MRCP
  • Endoscopic cannulation and contrast imaging of ducts
  • Stone, stricture or leak assessment during procedure
  • Biopsy or brushings in selected suspected strictures

Treatment Options

  • Bile duct stone removal
  • Sphincterotomy and balloon or basket extraction
  • Plastic or metal stent placement for obstruction
  • Management of selected bile leaks or strictures

How to Prepare

  • Do not eat or drink before the procedure as instructed
  • Tell the doctor about blood thinners, allergies and prior pancreatitis
  • Hospital observation may be advised after ERCP

Recovery & Follow-Up

Monitoring after ERCP checks for pain, fever, bleeding or pancreatitis. Some patients need admission, antibiotics or planned surgery after duct clearance.

URGENT CARE

Warning Signs That Should Not Wait

  • Severe abdominal pain after ERCP
  • Fever, chills or worsening jaundice
  • Vomiting blood or black stools
  • Persistent vomiting or fainting

This page is for patient education. A physical consultation is required to confirm diagnosis, risks, alternatives and the right treatment plan.
