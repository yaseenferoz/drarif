HEALTH TIPS

7 Warning Signs You Should Never Ignore About Your Digestive Health

Digestive symptoms are common, but some changes should not be dismissed as routine acidity or indigestion. Early evaluation can help identify ulcers, gallbladder disease, inflammatory bowel disease, liver or pancreatic problems and gastrointestinal cancers at a more treatable stage.

Persistent Pain

Ongoing abdominal pain, worsening cramps or pain after meals needs assessment.

Weight Loss

Unexplained weight loss, poor appetite or fatigue should be checked.

Bleeding

Blood in stool, black stools or repeated vomiting with blood requires urgent care.

Bowel Changes

New constipation, diarrhea, narrowing of stool or incomplete emptying may need colon evaluation.

Other warning signs include repeated vomiting, jaundice, difficulty swallowing and symptoms that wake you from sleep. A consultation helps decide whether blood tests, ultrasound, endoscopy, colonoscopy or imaging is appropriate.

What to Observe

  • How long symptoms have been present
  • Food triggers, fever, vomiting or jaundice
  • Weight loss, anemia, bleeding or appetite change

Tests May Include

  • Blood tests and liver function tests
  • Ultrasound, CT scan or MRI when needed
  • Endoscopy or colonoscopy for internal assessment

Seek Urgent Care

  • Severe persistent abdominal pain
  • Blood vomiting or black stools
  • Yellow eyes, high fever or repeated vomiting

PATIENT GUIDE

Gallstones: Symptoms, Causes and When Surgery Is Needed

Gallstones form inside the gallbladder and may remain silent for years. When they block the flow of bile, they can cause pain in the upper abdomen, nausea, vomiting, bloating after oily food, fever or jaundice.

Symptoms

Right upper abdominal pain, back or shoulder pain, fever, vomiting or yellow eyes.

Diagnosis

Ultrasound and blood tests help confirm stones and look for infection or bile duct blockage.

Treatment

Laparoscopic gallbladder removal is commonly advised for repeated painful attacks or complications.

Emergency review is important if gallstone pain is associated with fever, severe vomiting, jaundice or persistent pain, as these can indicate infection, pancreatitis or bile duct stones.

Why Tablets Usually Do Not Solve It

Painkillers and acidity medicines may temporarily reduce discomfort, but they do not remove stones or prevent serious complications such as infection, pancreatitis or bile duct blockage.

What Laparoscopic Surgery Means

For suitable patients, the gallbladder is removed through small cuts using a camera. Recovery is usually faster than open surgery, but the exact plan depends on inflammation, infection and patient fitness.

WELLNESS

Simple Lifestyle Changes for Better Gut Health Every Day

Gut health improves when daily routines support regular digestion, balanced meals, hydration, physical activity and sleep. These habits are useful for many people, but persistent symptoms still need proper medical evaluation.

  • Eat fiber-rich foods such as vegetables, fruits, pulses and whole grains as tolerated.
  • Drink enough water and avoid frequent dehydration, especially in hot weather.
  • Walk or exercise regularly to support bowel movement and metabolic health.
  • Limit smoking, excess alcohol and repeated self-medication for acidity or pain.
  • Seek care when acidity, constipation, diarrhea, pain or bleeding keeps recurring.

COMMON SYMPTOMS

Acidity, Heartburn and Reflux: When Should You See a Specialist?

Occasional acidity after heavy or spicy meals is common. Repeated heartburn, sour burps, chest burning, chronic cough, throat irritation or difficulty swallowing can suggest gastroesophageal reflux disease or another upper GI problem.

Triggers

Late meals, smoking, alcohol, obesity, stress, painkillers and certain foods may worsen reflux.

Evaluation

Persistent symptoms may need medication review, lifestyle correction and sometimes upper GI endoscopy.

Red Flags

Trouble swallowing, vomiting, bleeding, anemia or weight loss should be checked promptly.

Screening can prevent cancer
PREVENTION

Colonoscopy and Colon Cancer Screening: What Patients Should Know

Colonoscopy helps examine the large intestine and rectum. It can find causes of bleeding, bowel habit changes, anemia and abdominal symptoms, and it can also detect and remove some polyps before they become cancer.

Who May Need It

  • Blood in stool or unexplained anemia
  • New constipation or diarrhea pattern
  • Family history of colon cancer or polyps

How to Prepare

  • Follow bowel preparation instructions carefully
  • Share blood thinner and diabetes medicine details
  • Arrange a responsible attendant if sedation is used

The right timing for screening depends on age, symptoms, personal history and family history. A consultation helps decide whether colonoscopy, stool tests or imaging is most appropriate.
