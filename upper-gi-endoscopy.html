DIAGNOSTIC ENDOSCOPY

Upper GI Endoscopy in Kalaburagi

Flexible camera examination of the food pipe, stomach and duodenum for acidity, pain, vomiting, bleeding, difficulty swallowing and biopsy.

Upper GI endoscopy, also called EGD, uses a flexible camera to view the esophagus, stomach and duodenum. It helps diagnose ulcers, reflux complications, bleeding, narrowing, infection and suspected tumors.

The procedure can also take biopsies, treat bleeding or perform selected therapeutic steps when needed.

Book Consultation
WHEN TO CONSULT

Symptoms and Situations That Need Review

Persistent acidity, upper abdominal pain or vomiting

Difficulty swallowing or food sticking

Vomiting blood, black stools or anemia

Unexplained weight loss or appetite loss

Diagnosis & Evaluation

  • Direct camera inspection of the upper digestive tract
  • Biopsy for ulcers, infection, celiac disease or suspected cancer
  • Assessment of bleeding source or narrowing
  • Review of findings with treatment plan

Treatment Options

  • Medicines based on findings such as ulcer or reflux disease
  • Endoscopic bleeding control in selected cases
  • Dilatation or other therapy for selected narrowing
  • Surgery referral if tumor, perforation or complex disease is found

How to Prepare

  • Follow fasting instructions, commonly no food or drink for several hours
  • Tell the doctor about blood thinners, allergies and previous reactions to sedation
  • Arrange someone to accompany you if sedation is used

Recovery & Follow-Up

Mild throat discomfort or bloating can occur briefly. Biopsy results may take a few days. Follow post-procedure instructions before eating, driving or restarting medicines.

URGENT CARE

Warning Signs That Should Not Wait

  • Chest or abdominal pain that worsens after endoscopy
  • Vomiting blood or black stools
  • Fever, breathing difficulty or severe throat pain
  • Repeated vomiting after the procedure

This page is for patient education. A physical consultation is required to confirm diagnosis, risks, alternatives and the right treatment plan.
